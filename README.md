### GENE PHENOTYPE RELATIONSHIP:
Hemophilia A is an X-linked recessive bleeding disorder caused by mutations in the F8 gene, resulting in deficiency of clotting Factor VIII. It is characterized by persistent bleeding after trauma and hemarthroses (joint bleeds) with prolonged PTT, while platelet count and function remain normal. Female carriers have partial Factor VIII deficiency(he body has lower-than-normal levels of Factor VIII, a protein needed for proper blood clotting.) but are usually unaffected.

### Gene-Phenoype Relationship:
Tay-Sachs disease is an autosomal recessive lysosomal storage disorder caused by mutations in the HEXA gene, resulting in Hexosaminidase A deficiency and GM2-ganglioside accumulation in neurons. It presents in infancy with progressive neurodegeneration including psychomotor regression, seizures, hypotonia, blindness, and a characteristic cherry-red spot in the macula, and is usually fatal by age 5 years

### Gene-Phenotype Relationship:
Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis 1 (ALS1) is a progressive neurodegenerative disease caused by mutations in the SOD1 gene, where nerve cells controlling muscle movement gradually die. This leads to increasing muscle weakness, wasting, and paralysis, along with difficulty speaking and swallowing. About 10% of cases run in families, while the remaining 90% occur sporadically.

# Ellis-van Creveld Syndrome: <br>
<img width="811" height="407" alt="Screenshot 2026-02-28 172847" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/1d693045-1449-4630-a78e-77dc804af7b7" /> <br>

Ellis-van Creveld syndrome is an autosomal recessive skeletal dysplasia caused by mutations in the EVC or EVC2 genes, characterized by short-limb dwarfism, extra fingers/toes (polydactyly), nail dysplasia, and congenital heart defects (most commonly atrial septal defect). Additional features include dental abnormalities, narrow chest, and partial cleft lip, with increased frequency among the Amish population.
